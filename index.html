OUR PARTNERS

Our impact in Syria is built on deep, trusted relationships—with artisans, institutions, community leaders, and government officials who share our commitment to cultural renewal. At Syrian Mosaic Foundation, these partnerships are the foundation of every initiative we lead.

Local Artisan Cooperatives

We work directly with skilled craftsmen to sustain traditional Syrian arts—from mosaic work to textile weaving—ensuring ancient techniques survive and thrive for generations to come.

Cultural Institutions

Active partnerships with museums, universities, and cultural centers across Syria and internationally to document, protect, and amplify Syria's heritage on a global stage.

Community Leaders

A trusted network of local leaders, activists, and organizers who drive grassroots change—from heritage restoration to youth education—across Syrian communities.

SYRIA
BY NUMBERS
Discover Syria

6 UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Syria is home to six UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Ancient City of Damascus, Palmyra, Aleppo, Bosra, and Crac des Chevaliers—testaments to millennia of civilization.

10,000+ Years of History

Damascus is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, with evidence of settlement dating back over 10,000 years.

3 Major Religions' Holy Sites

Syria holds sacred sites for Christianity, Islam, and Judaism—including the Umayyad Mosque, Chapel of Ananias, and ancient synagogues of Damascus.
